Scrum

Scrum is an Agile framework for managing and completing complex projects.

Scrum was first described by Ken Schwaber and Jeff Sutherland in the mid-1990s.

Roles Scrum:

  • Product Owner
  • Scrum Master
  • Development Team
The Product Owner is responsible for the following functions:
  • Defining the features and requirements of the product
  • Prioritizing the backlog
  • Communicating with stakeholders
  • Making decisions
  • Managing the budget
  • Adjusting Sprint Goal
The Scrum Master is responsible for the following functions:
  • Facilitating Scrum ceremonies
  • Removing obstacles
  • Protecting the team
  • Coaching the team
  • Promoting transparency
  • Enforcing Scrum rules
  • Helping with Scaling
The Development Team is responsible for the following functions:
  • Delivering product functionality
  • Self-organization
  • Cross-functional
  • Estimating work

The meetings included in Scrum are:

  • Sprint Planning
  • Daily Scrum
  • Sprint Review
  • Sprint Retrospective

Sprint Planning

This meeting is held at the beginning of each Sprint to plan the work for the upcoming Sprint.

The Development Team, Product Owner, and Scrum Master attend this meeting.

Daily Scrum

This meeting is held every day and is used to plan the work for the day, update progress, and identify any issues that need to be addressed.

The Development Team attends this meeting.

Sprint Review

This meeting is held at the end of each Sprint to review the progress and provide feedback.

The Development Team, Product Owner, and Scrum Master attend this meeting.

Sprint Retrospective

This meeting is held at the end of each Sprint to evaluate the previous Sprint, identify areas of improvement, and plan actions to improve the next Sprint.

The Development Team, Product Owner, and Scrum Master attend this meeting.

Benefits of Scrum

  • Faster delivery
  • Increased flexibility
  • Improved collaboration
  • Greater accountability
  • Increased transparency
  • Better quality
  • Increased team motivation and satisfaction
  • Better alignment with business goals
